Events

In addition to the luncheon, organizations, universities and individuals throughout Evansville are planning special programs in celebration of this important milestone in the history of our democracy.

The League of Women Voters, Stepping Up and the Women’s Equality Day Committee are helping to organize and promote the events.

Events include:

  • February 9 – The League of Women Voters, which grew out of the suffrage movement, will celebrate its 100th birthday with an appearance by Elaine Weiss, author of the best-selling novel, “The Woman’s Hour," which is being turned into a Netflix series by Steven Spielberg. The program will be at. 2 p.m. at the Victory Theatre. Free but tickets are recommended by going to the League of Women Voters of Southwestern Indiana website.
  • February – The Athena Award by the Southwest Indiana Chamber will pay tribute to outstanding women.
  • March 4 – UE Andiron Lecture by Roberta Heiman on the Southwestern Indiana suffrage movement and the women who led it.
  • March 15 — A march in Downtown Evansville will be a re-enactment of the famous 1913 Woman’s Suffrage Parade in the nation’s capital. The march is sponsored by the Evansville Alumnae Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta.
  • May through August 2020 – Southwestern Indiana Master Gardeners will grow a “yellow garden” (display garden) to echo the “Yellow Rose” symbol of the women’s suffrage campaign the early 20th Century.
  • June 2 through 27 — Arts Council of Southwestern Indiana will sponsor an exhibition and reception to the theme of “Women have the vote, now what?”
  • August 26 – Women’s Equality Day luncheon, a celebration of the 100th anniversary of the ratification of the 19th Amendment on August 26, 1920
  • August 9 through October 25 — Evansville Museum of Arts, History and Science exhibit: “Votes for Women: Justice Delayed.”
  • October — Students of the University of Southern Indiana offering a conference to celebrate the 2020 anniversary of votes for women.
  • Fall 2020 — The Human Relations Commission Annual Dinner & Mayor’s Celebration of Diversity Awards

How people and organizations can help

In addition to attending the events, we encourage local organizations and employers to recognize the women who’ve been trailblazers in their own histories by posting tributes on their web sites and newsletters. Local families and individuals can research the lives and achievements of women in their own families, and share their stories on Facebook. The goal is to tell the untold story of women’s lives and contributions in our community’s history.
